# Crop Guide — What to Grow & When to Harvest

## Priority 1: Learn First (Weeks 1–6)

### Lettuce (Buttercrunch, Romaine, Red Leaf)
- **Days to harvest:** 30–45 days
- **Yield:** 5–6 oz per head, 3–5 regrows from outer leaf harvesting
- **pH:** 5.5–6.5 | **EC:** 1.2–1.8
- **Light:** 150–250 PPFD, 14–16 hrs
- **Why first:** Most forgiving crop, teaches every fundamental skill

### Basil (Genovese, Thai)
- **Days to harvest:** 28 days to first cut
- **Yield:** Continuous for 4–6 months with regular pinching
- **pH:** 5.5–6.5 | **EC:** 1.0–1.6
- **Light:** 200–300 PPFD, 14–16 hrs
- **Tip:** Pinch above leaf nodes to promote bushy growth

### Green Onions / Scallions
- **Days to harvest:** 21–28 days
- **Yield:** Regrows from base after cutting
- **pH:** 6.0–7.0 | **EC:** 1.4–1.8
- **Light:** 150–250 PPFD, 12–16 hrs

## Priority 2: Caribbean Essentials (Start in Month 2)

### Callaloo (Amaranth Greens) 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY
- **Days to harvest:** 20–45 days (first cut)
- **Yield:** Up to 10 harvests in 90 days (cut-and-come-again)
- **pH:** 5.5–6.5 | **EC:** 1.5–2.5
- **Light:** 200–400 PPFD, 14–16 hrs
- **System:** DWC or NFT
- **Notes:** Grows 30–50% faster than soil. 61% higher antioxidants hydroponically.
  Rise Gardens sells callaloo seed pods optimized for hydroponics.

### Culantro / Shadow Beni / Recao 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY
- **Days to harvest:** ~70 days from seed
- **Germination:** 14–30 days (slow! needs 80–85°F consistently)
- **pH:** 5.5–6.5 | **EC:** 1.0–1.8
- **Light:** 100–200 PPFD, 12–14 hrs (PREFERS SHADE — lower light = lower electricity)
- **System:** DWC or Kratky
- **Notes:** Nearly impossible to find fresh in Western MA.
  Natural shade preference makes it ideal for indoor growing.
  Once established, produces continuously for months.
  This is Pan Road Catering's biggest competitive advantage crop.

### Thyme (Common Thyme — Thymus vulgaris)
- **Days to harvest:** 30–60 days
- **Yield:** 386% more fresh weight vs. open-field growing
- **pH:** 5.5–7.0 | **EC:** 0.8–1.6
- **Light:** 200–400 PPFD, 14–16 hrs
- **Notes:** Compact, low-maintenance, long-lived

### Big Leaf Thyme (Cuban Oregano — Plectranthus amboinicus)
- **Days to harvest:** 30–45 days from cutting
- **pH:** 5.5–6.5 | **EC:** 1.0–1.5
- **Light:** 200–400 PPFD, 12–16 hrs
- **Notes:** NOT actually thyme — it's a different plant entirely.
  Essential for Caribbean cooking. Roots easily from cuttings in water.
  Thrives in warm, humid indoor conditions.

### Lemongrass
- **Days to harvest:** 60–90 days
- **Yield:** 92% more production than soil-grown
- **pH:** 5.5–6.5 | **EC:** 1.0–2.0
- **Light:** 300–500 PPFD, 14–16 hrs
- **Notes:** Start from grocery store stalks rooted in water.
  Needs larger containers (3–5 gallon DWC bucket).

## Priority 3: Fruiting (Start Month 3–4)

### Scotch Bonnet Peppers 🟡 MEDIUM PRIORITY
- **Days to harvest:** 90–150 days seed to ripe fruit
- **Yield:** 20–50+ peppers per plant per season
- **pH:** 5.5–6.5 | **EC:** 2.0–2.5
- **Light:** 400–600+ PPFD, 14–16 hrs veg / 12 hrs flower
- **System:** DWC in dedicated 5-gallon bucket
- **Temperature:** 75–95°F (these like HEAT)
- **Critical:** Hand-pollinate indoors with small brush or by shaking plants.
  Plants become MORE productive as perennials in year 2+.
  Start seeds NOW even if you're focused on greens — the 3-month lead time is real.

## Skip These Indoors

| Crop | Why Not |
|------|---------|
| Sorrel (hibiscus) | Grows 4–6 ft tall, needs massive light |
| Pimento / Allspice | It's a tree |
| Ginger / Turmeric | 6–18 months to produce usable rhizomes |
| Breadfruit / Ackee | Trees — not viable indoors |
