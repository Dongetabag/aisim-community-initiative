# System Specifications — Indoor Hydroponics

## Recommended Starter: Deep Water Culture (DWC)

DWC is the best all-around system for beginners. Plant roots sit in oxygenated nutrient
solution inside buckets, with an air pump providing constant oxygen. It handles everything
from lettuce to scotch bonnet peppers, scales by adding buckets, and teaches every
fundamental skill.

## Equipment List

### Core System
- **4x 5-gallon buckets** with 6" net pot lids (VIVOSUN or DIY) — $50–$80
- **Air pump** — Active Aqua or VIVOSUN 4-outlet — $20–$30
- **Air stones** — 4" cylinder, 4-pack — $8–$10
- **Air tubing** — 25 ft standard — $5–$8

### Lighting
- **Spider Farmer SF2000** — 200W, Samsung diodes, dimmable, covers 2×4' — $140–$150
- **BN-LINK mechanical timer** — $8–$12
- Settings: 14–16 hours on for greens/herbs, 12 hours for flowering peppers

### Nutrients & Testing
- **General Hydroponics Flora Trio** (FloraMicro, FloraGro, FloraBloom) 3 quarts — $30–$40
- **pH meter + TDS/EC meter** — VIVOSUN combo — $12–$20
- **GH pH Control Kit** (pH Up + pH Down) — $12–$18

### Growing Medium
- **LECA clay pebbles** — 10L bag (GROW!T or generic) — $15–$20
- Alternative: Lava rocks from landscaping supplier — $3–10/cu ft (best for ebb-and-flow)

### Environment
- **6" oscillating clip fan** — $15–$20
- **Thermometer/hygrometer** — $8–$12

### Seeds (Starter Selection)
- Lettuce (Buttercrunch, Romaine, Red Leaf mix)
- Basil (Genovese, Thai)
- Callaloo / Amaranth greens
- Culantro / Recao / Shadow Beni
- Scotch Bonnet pepper
- Thyme (common + Cuban oregano/big leaf thyme)
- Total seed cost: $15–$25

## Space Requirements

**Phase 1:** 2×4 feet (8 sq ft) — spare room corner, closet, or basement
**Phase 2:** 4×4 feet (16 sq ft) — add second system + light
**Phase 3:** 50–100 sq ft — dedicated grow room

## Growing Medium Comparison

| Medium | Cost | Best System | Reusable | Notes |
|--------|------|-------------|----------|-------|
| LECA (clay pebbles) | $8–15/cu ft | DWC, Ebb-Flow | Yes | Smooth, gentle on roots, beginner-friendly |
| Lava rock | $3–10/cu ft | Ebb-Flow, DWC | Yes | Cheap, great drainage, sharp edges damage roots |
| Rockwool | $12–18/98 cubes | NFT, Drip | No | Industry standard for seed starting |
| Perlite | $5–7/cu ft | Drip blends | Limited | Floats in flood systems, dusty |
| Coco coir | $5–12/cu ft | Drip | No | Best water retention, needs cal-mag supplement |

### Lava Rock Tips
- Select smaller, tumbled pieces to reduce root damage
- Rinse thoroughly until water runs clear before use
- Test pH before adding (most are neutral, some run alkaline)
- Best match is ebb-and-flow systems where flooding/draining complements drainage
