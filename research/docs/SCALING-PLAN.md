# Scaling Plan — From Hobby to Catering Supply

## Phase 1: Learn the Basics (Months 1–2)
**Investment:** $345–$456  
**Plant sites:** 8–12  
**Weekly yield:** 1–2 lbs greens  
**Focus:** Lettuce + basil to master pH, nutrients, harvest timing  
**Action:** Start scotch bonnet seeds NOW (3–4 month lead time)

## Phase 2: Diversify (Months 3–6)
**Add:** $200–$400  
**Plant sites:** 20–40  
**Weekly yield:** 3–5 lbs  
**New equipment:**
- Second system (VIVOSUN NFT $79 or Mr. Stacky tower $110–170)
- Second light (Spider Farmer SF1000 ~$80)
**Crops:** Add callaloo, culantro, thyme, green onions  
**Cumulative investment:** $560–$880

## Phase 3: Catering Supply (Months 6–12)
**Add:** $500–$2,000  
**Plant sites:** 50–100+  
**Weekly yield:** 10–20 lbs + peppers  
**Space:** Dedicated spare room or large closet (50–100 sq ft)  
**Equipment:** 3–4 LED panels (600–800W total)  
**Monthly electricity:** $80–$112  
**Cumulative investment:** $1,000–$2,500

## Phase 4: Evaluate Commercial (12+ Months)
**Investment:** $3,000–$10,000+  
**Trigger:** Consistently producing 15+ lbs/week AND demand exceeds capacity  
**Considerations:**
- Dedicated grow room conversion
- MA "Right to Farm" laws protect agricultural activities
- Fresh uncut produce requires no special food manufacturing permit
- "Grown in-house" marketing angle for Pan Road Catering

## Economics at Scale

| Ingredient | Market Price | Your Cost | Savings |
|------------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Fresh basil | $6–12/lb wholesale | $2–4/lb | 50–67% |
| Callaloo | $8–15/lb (when available) | $3–5/lb | 60–67% |
| Culantro | $10–20/lb (rare in MA) | $3–5/lb | 70–75% |
| Scotch bonnet | $8–15/lb | $2–4/lb | 67–73% |
