# Accessibility Adaptations for Elderly & Disability Access

## Design Principles
This system is designed so anyone can grow food regardless of mobility,
vision, or physical strength. Every recommendation below keeps the
system manageable for someone who may have limited movement, arthritis,
low vision, or who uses a wheelchair/walker.

## Height & Reach Adaptations

### Raised System Placement
- Place DWC buckets on a sturdy table or shelf at 30–36" height (wheelchair accessible)
- Avoid floor-level setups that require bending or kneeling
- Vertical towers should be no taller than 5 feet — keep all plant sites within arm's reach
- Use a rolling cart ($20–30) so the system can be moved to the person

### Lightweight Components
- Use 3.5-gallon buckets instead of 5-gallon (weighs 29 lbs full vs 42 lbs)
- Keep a small watering can nearby for top-offs (avoid carrying heavy containers)
- Use a siphon pump ($10) to drain reservoirs without lifting

## Low-Vision Adaptations
- Label everything with large-print waterproof labels
- Use color-coded buckets (green = greens, red = peppers, blue = herbs)
- Digital pH meters with large backlit displays (VIVOSUN brand works well)
- Set phone alarms for light schedules if not using a timer
- Talking kitchen scale for measuring nutrients

## Reduced Dexterity / Arthritis
- Use syringes with easy-grip handles for nutrient measuring (not tiny droppers)
- Pre-mix nutrients in larger batches — store a week's worth in a gallon jug
- Velcro plant ties instead of twist ties
- Electric water pump for reservoir changes instead of manual siphoning
- Ergonomic pruning shears with spring-loaded handles

## Simplified System: The Kratky "Set and Forget"
For the most hands-off experience, the Kratky method is ideal:
- No pump, no electricity (except the light), no moving parts
- Fill container with nutrient solution, place plant, walk away
- Check once a week — just top off water if needed
- Best for: lettuce, basil, herbs, green onions
- A single Kratky mason jar setup costs under $5 per plant

## Recommended Starter for Limited Mobility
1. Wire shelving unit on wheels ($40) at waist height
2. 6–8 Kratky mason jars with net pot lids ($30)
3. Barrina T5 LED strip lights mounted on shelf above ($50)
4. Pre-mixed nutrient jugs (mix once per month, pour as needed)
5. Total setup: ~$150 and minimal ongoing effort

## Community Support Model
- Pair each grower with a "grow buddy" who can help with:
  - Monthly nutrient mixing
  - Reservoir deep-cleans every 2 weeks
  - Equipment troubleshooting
  - Harvesting and seed starting
- Create a WhatsApp/group chat for photos and questions
- Monthly community check-in (virtual or in-person)

## Safety Notes
- LED grow lights can be bright — don't stare directly at them
- Keep electrical connections away from water (use GFCI outlets)
- pH Up/Down solutions are caustic — wear gloves, store safely
- Nutrient solutions are non-toxic but may stain clothing
- Keep a towel nearby — small spills are normal and expected
